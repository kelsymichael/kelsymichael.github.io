Redesign

problem

SCHAWEL+COLES wanted to see how I would approach redesigning a section of one of their client’s current site.

constraints

  • Time (1 day)
  • No client interaction

Process Overview

I work in a ‘Lean UX’ method of Build -> Measure -> Learn, and quick iterations.

The time constraint of 24 hours, and not being able to interact with the client, I made some assumptions that I would have first discussed with the client and tested afterwards to verify success.

Whatchu workin’ with

Site Audit

Paper

In order to find out what area would have the most impact from a redesign, I started with taking a general audit of the site.

For easy annotation and the ability to have various type of media without looking terrible (i.e., g-docs), I chose to use Dropbox's Paper to take my notes.

Included

  • current information architecture
  • basic QA (links/buttons/forms)
  • website responsiveness
  • screenshots
  • date of content
  • pagespeed insights
  • problem code snippets

Outcome Problem Solution

Events Page

After the site audit, I decided to focus on the ‘Events’ page. By focusing on an outcome, I approached the redesign with the intent to increase signups for race events.

The current website had several areas that I could reasonably assume were impacting sign ups.

Going with the outcome of 'increasing event sign ups,' I would have nailed down a meaningful percentage increase with the client.

Conclusion

RaceTri Events Redesign

contstraints

Not being able to talk to the client gave me a chance to examine the website in its entirety, without having any assumptions of 'what' to redesign.

I believe it made my audit a little bit more thorough and I was also able to take on the website as a whole and determine what I thought had the most potential for impact.

process

I work in a cross-functional team and have the luxury of a short feedback loop from developers and PM's, and in this case (working solo) I did my best to think outside of my role.

Taking into account development concerns and business requirements (even imaginary) was a good exercise.

lessons learned

The short timeframe meant I had to be ruthless with my time management, and in hindsight, I should have been faster with my audit and not have spent time prototyping a button interaction.

I spent a little bit of time in Framer prototyping what a register-button -> inline form would work, but I think time could have been better spent making the header/footer/sponsor section more refined.
